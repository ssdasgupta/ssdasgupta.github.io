Bio

I am currently a 5th-year Ph.D. student in the College of Information and Computer Sciences at UMass Amherst, working with Prof. Andrew McCallum at IESL.
My research is focused on developing set-based representation learning techniques, with applications in natural language processing and information retrieval. I earned my Master’s degree from the Indian Institute of Science (IISc) in 2018, where I was part of the Machine and Language Learning (MALL) Lab, working with Prof. Partha Talukdar. In addition to my academic research, I have gained valuable industry experience through work/internships with leading researchers, including Dr. Tijmen Tieleman at Minds.ai, Dr. Steffen Rendle at Google Research, Dr. Achille Fokoue at IBM Research, and Dr. Tobias Schnabel at Microsoft Research.

Research Interests

  • Set-Theoretic Tasks: My research focuses on improving the ability of models to handle compositional queries involving set operations like intersection, union, and negation. In my work Answering Compositional Queries with Set-Theoretic Embeddings, I introduced a benchmark to evaluate the performance of models on complex set-theoretic tasks, showing significant improvements over existing methods.
  • Set-Theoretic Embeddings: I have developed geometric embeddings that capture the semantics of sets using box-based representations. My work on Word2Box demonstrates how box embeddings can model set-theoretic properties like hierarchy and inclusion. Additionally, in Box-To-Box Transformations for Modeling Joint Hierarchies, I proposed techniques to enhance the expressiveness of box embeddings for hierarchical structures.
  • Graph Representation Learning: I have explored various approaches to improving graph-based models. In HyTE: Hyperplane-based Temporally aware Knowledge Graph Embedding, I introduced a temporal extension to knowledge graph embeddings, enhancing their ability to handle time-sensitive data. My work on Graph Convolution Networks for Document Dating applies graph-based models to real-world NLP tasks, showing improvements in document temporal ordering.
  • Temporal Learning: I have worked on integrating temporal reasoning into machine learning models. In HyTE, I proposed a hyperplane-based approach to model the temporal evolution of knowledge graphs. Additionally, in AD3: Attentive Deep Document Dater, I developed methods to predict document dates by combining graph-based and attention-based models.
